What I built for this proposal

I developed this proposal independently for a government agency engagement. The work involved more than writing a document — I had to understand the client's operational need (certifying personnel as SOC analysts), select and sequence the right curriculum modules from the Cisco CyberOps Associate program, design a schedule that caused zero weekday disruption to government staff, build equipment packages at two price tiers based on existing branch infrastructure, and justify the pricing against the organization's internal benchmark formula before submitting for HQ approval.

The pricing justification was a specific requirement — I had to demonstrate that the ₱250,000 package was correctly priced relative to both the standard individual rate and the internal educator cost benchmark. The proposal was submitted with a recommendation to approve, which it received.

This type of work — translating a client's operational need into a structured, costed, schedulable program — is the same analytical thinking I bring to operations and VA work: understand the goal, build the structure, justify the numbers, get it approved.
